CrewSync — Coach Quick Start Guide

Get your program running in under 10 minutes

FREE DURING BETA

Welcome, Coach.

This guide walks you through the five core steps to get your program set up on CrewSync — from creating your account to managing your first practice. The whole process takes under 10 minutes.

1

Create Your Account

Go to crewsync.app/auth/signup and sign up with your email. Select "Coach" when asked about your role.

  • Enter your name, program name, and team sport (rowing).
  • No credit card required — everything is free during beta.
  • You'll land on your coach dashboard immediately after signup.
Tip: Use your school or club email for easy athlete verification later.
2

Set Up Your Roster

Navigate to Team → Athletes in the sidebar. Add athletes one of two ways:

  • Invite by email: Athletes receive a link to join your team automatically.
  • Manual entry: Add athlete names and weights directly — useful before athletes create accounts.
Tip: Athletes can connect their Concept2 logbook after joining so their erg data syncs automatically.
3

Build Your First Lineup

Go to Lineup Builder. You can drag and drop athletes into boat seats, or use the seat racing tool to compare pairs.

  • Set seat positions (bow, 2, 3 … stroke, cox) for any shell type.
  • Save multiple lineup scenarios — useful when you're deciding between boats.
  • Share a read-only lineup link with athletes or parents.
4

Schedule a Practice

Go to Calendar and click any date to create a practice session.

  • Set session type (on water, erg, weights, cross-training).
  • Attach an erg workout — athletes will see it in their dashboard.
  • Enable attendance tracking so you know who showed up.
Tip: The load management view lets you see weekly training load per athlete at a glance — great for avoiding overtraining before regattas.
5

Review Your Dashboard

Your coach dashboard shows you everything in one view:

  • Team health: Wellness scores and load indicators.
  • Erg leaderboard: Live rankings from Concept2 data.
  • Upcoming sessions: What's on the calendar this week.
  • Alerts: Athletes with high load or missed attendance.

Ready to get started?
